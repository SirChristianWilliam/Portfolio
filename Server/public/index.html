My dad died from ALS in 2020. I spent countless hours researching this disease. However much I looked, I was always left wanting. What I realized is that there is nowhere that explains anything about the person who has ALS. There are symptoms, and plenty of websites telling you how there's no cure, but nothing to draw any real conclusions of what could potentially cause it in the first place. That's why I created Missing Link. Any user who has been diagnosed with a disease or condition will be given an access code from their doctor. On my website, they will then search up the condition they have and enter the code for it. The user will have a profile page where they can navigate to the questions page. This page is intended to have hundreds if not thousands of questions. The questions encompass the user's symptoms, but more importantly, data about the user themselves. Hair color, what city they grew up, how many hours of sleep they get per night, do they drink coffee, or alcohol, or smoke marijuana. Individually, these questions are seemingly unimportant. However, if hundreds of thousands of users are diagnosed with ALS and answer these questions, there is a possibility of discovering a connection, or "missing link" between users for that particular condition. On the results page, if one user's question/answer pair is identical to another user's question/answer pair, that row will be combined and the match count will increase by 1. This allows compression of the data and provides an excellent filter to compare amongst the users. This app has a lot of real-world potential, and I do hope to expand on it over the years until it's production ready. Imagine if 90% of those with ALS had all started drinking alcohol before the age of ten. How would we know this? Why would anyone even think to ask that question of each patient? Missing Link is aptly named as it's looking for just that, for finding those odd connections in an attempt to lead researchers in new directions, as the old ones haven't been working.

This project meant a lot to me because I have friends and family who are part of the LGBTQ+ community, and unfortunately, discrimination and hate is still a part of our society. I chose to work with Queerspace to help them streamline their current functionalities and processes into a singular entity. The main benifit of our app is to allow the staff at Queerspace have more control over registering users, creating/editing/deleting events, and incorporating other resources from their current website on ours, so users and staff will not have to keep navigating between the two, and instead can do it all here. Presenting:
Netclips

Ah yes, Netclips. This project functions as a movie view & add program. It heavily relies on Redux, Sagas, React, and postgreSQL, and focuses on multiple tables requiring "many-to-many" relationships to process the data correctly. This project was very beneficial for preparing me for my project "Missing Link", as it "Missing Link" deals with very complicated "many-to-many" relationships. I am very happy with how this app turned out, functionality aside. The styling looks great and the hover/active effects I've included really flesh it out as a whole. The background header color subtly changes tones over a short period of time, but if you're not looking for it you might miss it! It's also viewport responsive. I try to build single-page-applications with the user experience in mind, first and foremost, and you can definitely feel that when interacting on this application. (You also may notice the genres for many of the titles are not correct. That's okay. Art is subjective).
